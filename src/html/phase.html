USER INTERFACE CASE STUDY

Phase Robotics - User Interface

CHAPTER 1

Research &
Development

The approach to the user interface for Phase took careful planning and extensive research to reach the best design solution. It is a very specific product, and as such, the design philosophy must cohere with the users' needs. User engagement and success within the application is pivotal to design orientation.

My research started looking at competition and what they have done to create a successful product with a satisfied and engaging community of users, that all contribute to success. A heavy emphasis on community collaboration puts even more emphasis on the user experience being thoroughly enjoyable and easy to use.

The complexity of the application means that it is easy for the design to become crowded and confusing, something that is important to avoid for good user experience. This is why I created clear sections in the design demonstrated with visual elements, it breaks up the complexity and allows the user to easily digest the relevant information.

CHAPTER 2

Colour Palette

The colour palette was determined by the clients' request for a dark theme. Dark themes can be interesting and visually unique, but they also come with limitations and things to consider; such as difficulty reading non-primary text elements, due to contrast issues. A darker tone can cause certain elements to get washed out in the design, such as buttons and other important elements.

To compensate for the issues that can be had with a dark theme, I decided to use a blend of dark and light themes to create an interesting balance between colours that allow for easy viewing and accessibility.

CHAPTER 3

Typeface

Typeface choice was another carefully deliberated factor that took significant thought and usability testing. Initially, I wanted a typeface that was unique but still included the necessary components of modern interface design.

Proxima Nova turned out to be the perfect choice for this interface as it has lots of visual quirks in varying stroke sizes on curved letters, whilst maintaining legibility and that classic modern interface typeface look. It was also important that it fits well with other fonts used throughout the website, and due to its excellent versatility, they can work in unison without looking out of place or neglected.
